How to Play

Anti-Magic Squares

  • Fill the empty cells using each missing number exactly once.
  • Add up every row, every column and the two long diagonals. These are the line sums.
  • Unlike a normal magic square, the line sums should not all match.
  • They must make a run of consecutive numbers. On Easy, the ten line sums must be 30, 31, 32, 33, 34, 35, 36, 37, 38 and 39, each used once.
  • No row or column owns a particular target. R1 does not have to be 30, C2 does not have to be 31, and so on. Any line may take any unused target total.
  • The tracker shows each row, column and diagonal sum. Red means two lines have the same sum. Gold means the final list has a gap.

Alphanametics

  • Place the nine given numbers into a 3 by 3 grid.
  • The numbers must form a normal magic square.
  • The letter counts of their British-English names must also form a magic square.
  • Spaces and hyphens are ignored. British "and" is counted.

Controls

Tap a cell and then a number, or drag a number from the tray. Use Hint, Check, Undo and Clear from the bottom bar.
